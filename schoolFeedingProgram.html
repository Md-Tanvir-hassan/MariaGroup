Primary & Mass Education – Feeding Programme

Supporting Education Through Nutritious School Feeding

At Maria Groups we are honored to play a vital role in the Government Primary School Feeding Programme, an initiative financed and supervised by the Ministry of Primary and Mass Education, Bangladesh. This national programme is designed to enhance the well-being of young learners by providing them with fresh, hygienic, and nutritious food on a regular basis.

We proudly supply essential food items to government primary schools across multiple Upazilas, including Bakerganj, Banaripara, Gaurnadi, Hizla, Muladi, Wazirpur, Kathalia, Nalchity, and many others across Bangladesh. Our service coverage ensures that thousands of children receive the nourishment they need to stay healthy, attentive, and motivated in their classrooms.

Program Expansion and Features

📅 Timeline & Budget

The government approved a new, expanded program in March 2025 to reintroduce school meals after a temporary end in 2022. The government has allocated Tk 5,452 crore for the three-year project, which began in mid-2025.

🎯 Target Population

The upazilas were chosen based on a 2022 poverty mapping survey, focusing on the most food-insecure regions. The program will also cover Rohingya children in Cox's Bazar and Bhasan Char.

🏛️ Implementation

The Directorate of Primary Education (DPE) implements the program, with strict monitoring by Upazila Nirbahi Officers (UNOs) to prevent food from being resold.

🌾 Local Economic Development

The program is designed to support local food systems and economies, potentially through procurement from local farmers.

Rationale and Benefits

🎓

Incentivizing Education

The food serves as an incentive for poor and vulnerable families to send their children to school and keep them enrolled.

🍎

Improving Nutrition

Providing fortified snacks and meals helps address high malnutrition rates among students, which can hinder academic performance.

📚

Boosting Learning

Well-fed students are less hungry, can concentrate better, and have improved learning abilities. Studies in Bangladesh have linked school feeding to reduced anemia.

📈

Attendance & Retention

The program aims to boost school attendance and reduce dropout rates, ensuring children stay in school longer.

Our Commitment to Excellence

We maintain strict quality control measures throughout the supply chain—from sourcing to delivery—to guarantee that every meal is safe, hygienic, and nutritious. Our team works closely with local authorities, schools, and programme supervisors to ensure smooth operations and timely supply.
