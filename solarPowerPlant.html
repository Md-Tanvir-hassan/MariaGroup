A New Solar Landmark in Cox's Bazar

Maria Groups' 70 MW Solar Power Plant at Chakaria

Maria Groups' Strategic Contribution to expand solar power capacity

Project Overview

In its latest push for sustainable energy, Bangladesh aims to significantly expand its solar power capacity. Among several major solar tenders issued, a standout proposal is for a 70 MW solar power plant to be sited in Chakaria, Cox's Bazar. The undertaking—planned by Maria Groups—promises to be a key contributor to the region's renewable energy infrastructure, aligning with national and global goals for clean power.

National Context and Significance

Bangladesh has recently moved to issue large tenders for grid-connected solar power plants, aiming to add nearly 1.78 gigawatts (GW) of solar capacity at multiple sites. The tender includes four 70 MW plants (including Chakaria) and fifteen 100 MW plants spread across various districts.

Chakaria's location in Cox's Bazar is strategic due to solar radiation levels, relatively flat lands, and increasing energy demand in the southeastern region.

Government's Broader Ambitions:

  • Expanding renewable energy in the generation mix
  • Reducing dependency on fossil fuels
  • Meeting climate targets and international commitments
  • Strengthening energy security, particularly in vulnerable coastal and off-grid regions

Technical & Economic Considerations

Detailed project planning covers multiple aspects to ensure successful implementation and operation

🌍 Site Selection

Ensuring minimal shading, stable land, local infrastructure (roads, accessibility), and proximity to grid substations to reduce transmission losses and costs.

🏞️ Land Use & Acquisition

Acquiring sufficient land with negotiations involving local landowners, environmental clearance, and minimal resettlement impact.

⚙️ Technology

Photovoltaic (PV) panels (mono- or poly-crystalline), inverters, mounting structures, and balance-of-system components with focus on efficiency and durability.

🔌 Grid Connection

Substation connectivity to tie the plant to the national grid, ensuring voltage regulation and system stability.

💰 Financing & Tariffs

Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) with BPDB over 20 years at a fixed rate per kWh, with funding from equity, loans, and international climate funds.

🌱 Environmental Impact

Minimizing impact on flora, fauna, and water drainage while addressing local concerns and creating employment opportunities.
